About This Agreement
What is the Operating Solution Agreement (OSA)?
The OSA is SaveMoneyCutCarbon's Sustainability-As-A-Service model. SMCC designs, funds, installs, owns, and maintains the energy assets at your premises. You pay a fixed monthly OSA payment and benefit from improved energy performance from day one — with no capital expenditure, no maintenance liability, and full performance-risk transfer to SMCC.

The OSA is structured as a service agreement, not a loan or borrowing arrangement. For academy trusts and local authority bodies, this means it is fully compliant with the DfE Academy Trust Handbook and does not appear on your balance sheet as a capital commitment.

How the OSA Works

SMCC's Responsibilities
  • Design & engineer the full solution
  • Fund and install all energy assets
  • Own the installed equipment throughout the term
  • Maintain, repair, and replace as required
  • Provide remote monitoring & performance reporting
  • Carry full operational & performance risk
  • Provide insurance-backed performance guarantee
Your Responsibilities
  • Grant access to premises for installation
  • Pay the agreed monthly OSA payment
  • Use the assets as intended
  • Notify SMCC of any faults or access issues

Terms & Conditions
1. Definitions

"Agreement" means this Operating Solution Agreement including all schedules. "Assets" means the energy equipment installed by SMCC at the Site. "OSA Payment" means the monthly service charge payable by the Customer. "SMCC" means Intelligent Resource Management Limited, trading as SaveMoneyCutCarbon. "Customer" means the organisation named in the Agreement Details. "Site" means the premises identified in the Solution Schedule. "Term" means the fixed period specified in the Commercial Terms.

2. Nature of the Agreement

This Agreement is a service agreement. SMCC provides energy services to the Customer, including design, installation, ownership, operation, maintenance, and performance management of the Assets. This Agreement does not constitute a lease, hire-purchase, finance arrangement, or borrowing by the Customer.

3. SMCC's Obligations

SMCC shall: (a) design and install the Assets to industry standards; (b) retain ownership of all Assets throughout the Term; (c) maintain, repair, and where necessary replace the Assets at its own cost; (d) provide SolarCare monitoring and performance reporting as described in the Solution Schedule; (e) carry all operational risk for the Assets; and (f) maintain adequate insurance throughout the Term.

4. Customer's Obligations

The Customer shall: (a) provide SMCC with reasonable access to the Site for installation, maintenance, and inspection; (b) pay the monthly OSA Payment by the agreed method; (c) use the Assets in accordance with SMCC's reasonable instructions; (d) promptly notify SMCC of any fault, damage, or access issue; and (e) not tamper with, modify, or encumber the Assets.

5. OSA Payments

The Customer shall pay the monthly OSA Payment as specified in the Solution Schedule, payable in advance on the first business day of each month. Payments shall escalate annually in accordance with the agreed escalation basis. SMCC reserves the right to charge interest at 3% per annum above the Bank of England base rate on overdue amounts.

6. Ownership of Assets

All Assets installed by SMCC at the Site remain the sole property of SMCC throughout the Term. The Customer shall not sell, transfer, encumber, or otherwise deal with the Assets. SMCC shall mark Assets with identification plates or labels where reasonably practical.

7. Performance Guarantee

Where a guaranteed annual output or saving is specified in the Solution Schedule, SMCC shall use commercially reasonable endeavours to achieve such guarantee. Where measured performance falls below the guaranteed level in any measurement period, SMCC shall apply the agreed remedy. SMCC's liability for underperformance shall not exceed the total OSA Payments received in the relevant measurement period.

8. Maintenance & Access

SMCC shall carry out all necessary maintenance, servicing, and repair of the Assets. The Customer shall provide reasonable access during normal working hours (and outside normal hours in the case of emergency) upon reasonable notice. Emergency access may be required for safety-critical interventions with minimal notice.

9. Insurance

SMCC shall maintain: (a) public liability insurance of not less than £10,000,000 per occurrence; (b) professional indemnity insurance of not less than £5,000,000; and (c) such other insurances as are required by law. The Customer is responsible for insuring its own property and the structural elements of the Site.

10. Data & Monitoring

SMCC shall have the right to collect, process, and use performance and consumption data generated by the Assets for the purposes of monitoring, reporting, and improving service delivery. SMCC shall handle all personal data in accordance with applicable data protection legislation. Anonymised performance data may be used by SMCC for benchmarking and product development.

11. Variations

Any material change to the Solution Schedule, OSA Payment, or Term must be agreed in writing and signed by authorised representatives of both parties. SMCC may make minor technical substitutions to the Assets where equivalent or superior equipment is provided, subject to prior written notification to the Customer.

12. Term & Termination

This Agreement shall commence on the Commencement Date and continue for the Fixed Term unless earlier terminated. Either party may terminate if the other is in material breach and fails to remedy such breach within 30 days of written notice. SMCC may terminate immediately if the Customer enters administration, liquidation, or is otherwise unable to pay its debts.

13. End of Term

Upon expiry of the Term, the Assets shall be dealt with in accordance with the End of Term provision in the Solution Schedule. Where assets transfer to the Customer, SMCC shall provide a written transfer of title. Where SMCC removes the Assets, removal shall be carried out at SMCC's cost and the Site returned to a reasonable condition.

14. Force Majeure

Neither party shall be liable for failure to perform its obligations due to events beyond its reasonable control including, but not limited to, acts of God, pandemic, war, strike, grid failure, or regulatory change. The affected party shall notify the other promptly and shall use reasonable endeavours to resume performance.

15. Limitation of Liability

Neither party shall be liable to the other for indirect, special, or consequential loss. SMCC's total liability under this Agreement in any 12-month period shall not exceed the total OSA Payments received in that period, save in respect of death or personal injury caused by negligence, or fraud.

16. Confidentiality

Each party shall keep confidential the commercial terms of this Agreement and any proprietary technical information disclosed by the other party. This obligation shall survive termination of this Agreement for a period of three years. Neither party shall issue press releases or public announcements regarding this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other.

17. Assignment

The Customer may not assign its rights or obligations under this Agreement without SMCC's prior written consent. SMCC may assign this Agreement to an affiliate or successor entity, or to a funder providing financing for the Assets, provided that such assignment does not adversely affect the Customer's rights under the Agreement.

18. Governing Law & Dispute Resolution

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales. In the event of a dispute, the parties shall first seek to resolve the matter through good-faith negotiation. Failing resolution within 30 days, either party may refer the matter to mediation before resorting to litigation.

19. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements. No amendment shall be effective unless made in writing and signed by authorised representatives of both parties.

20. Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect. The parties shall use reasonable endeavours to replace any invalid provision with a valid provision that achieves the same commercial purpose.

21. Academy Trust & Public Sector Compliance

For academy trusts and other public sector bodies, SMCC confirms that this Agreement is structured as a service agreement and is not a leasing, borrowing, or financial instrument arrangement within the meaning of the DfE Academy Trust Handbook or CIPFA guidelines. Monthly OSA Payments are treated as operational expenditure. SMCC will provide supporting documentation to assist with any required board or trustee approvals upon request. SMCC's retained ownership of Assets ensures this Agreement does not create a capital liability on the Customer's balance sheet.
